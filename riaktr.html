Title

Riaktr's SaaS platform assists managers in the telecommunications industry in Africa and Latin America to improve their distribution and sales management. The web app enables managers to monitor their team, assign tasks, manage inventory and access important metrics related to their points of sale. The user interface is designed to provide users with data-driven insights to make informed decisions.

As the first designer on the team, I collaborated with a small cross-functional agile team (six developers and one product manager) to redesign the software and improve key user flows. Conducting remote user research and taking into account legacy and technical constraints, I worked closely with the team to establish a shared vision and implement it incrementally. I also focused on improving the visual consistency of the app, and testing new design concepts to improve visibility of information on a data-heavy interface.

Additionally, I identified opportunities to improve the design process and led the implementation of those changes, including creating and promoting a design system, increasing the visibility of user research, and aligning design efforts with developers' sprints.

A massive block thorough this process has been the lack of adequate design components. The creation of new UI patterns ”on the fly” lead to both inconsistencies in the product and wasted time within the team. Before moving forward too fast, especially with the creation of the new  POI explorer, I would strongly recommend taking a step back, assessing our various components, as well as our key use cases, and taking the time to create a flexible and adapted components library, together with design and development, with the vision of slowly moving towards a design system, that would ultimately enable a much faster design-to-development process. More specifically, we wanted to offer a clear, functional and personalized dashboard to facilitate users’ workflow by: highlighting urgent actions that need taking care of, allowing them to quickly complete frequently accessed tasks, enabling them to monitor key information at a glance and offering curated and relevant content. Our goal was to increase users’ satisfaction and loyalty by, on one hand, making the executing staff more successful in their role and, on the other hand, providing value and creating trust for the decision makers. We would know this to be true when seeing an increase in both the frequency and depth of use of BelfiusWeb.








CO-CREATION

I had to do a lot of convincing and stakeholder management to launch this project, as this was not on the roadmap, and Belfius was still operating in a top-down approach when it came to design priorities.

To do so, I involved the whole team, as well as external experts (corporate bankers and customer support team) in a process of co-creation.In total, I facilitated 4 workshops to: define the business value of the idea (impact map), better understand the users’ needs and workflow (provisional personas), generate ideas and determine the ideal vision state, prioritize our ideas and agree on intermediary steps (impact vs effort matrix)







At the end of the second exercice, we had 3 profiles with specific but different tasks and needs, yet all using the same tool with the same request: to only see what’s relevant for them, with as little unnecessary steps and clutter as possible.

We therefore moved to the ideation exercice with those questions in mind:

  • How might we facilitate the users’ recurring tasks?
  • How might we meet the users’ personalization needs?
  • How might we prompt the users to connect every day to BelfiusWeb?
  • How might we continuously provide content that’s relevant to each user?


After the final workshop, we had defined the vision and quick-wins. While the goal was to provide relevant content and anticipate users' needs, we would start with a dashboard that could be manually personalized and that'd include: shortcuts to top tasks, recent activity (alerts, notifications, documents to sign,…), an overview on relevant accounts and communications from the bank. The second step would be to offer decision makers an actionable overview of the company's financial health and growth with relevant KPIs.

I went back to my laptop to design the initial concepts. There was a lot of back and forth over the next couple of months. I collaborated with the marketing team and UI designer Christian Swinnen to arrive at the final UI, as well as the research team to verify our assumptions and better define users' key tasks and workflows. I also worked closely with IT and Data to ensure the solution was future-proof. And I had to frequently validate the direction with the management team, as well as with the legal and security teams. Until, at last the final version was launched, with a lot of positive feedback from the users.

LEARNINGS

My mission at Belfius lasted a year and it is to date the one I found the most interesting and challenging. Processes were slow, the meetings long, the politics complicated and I had to work hard to prove the value of shifting towards a more user-centric approach. But looking back, I thrived in the complexity and the autonomy, I got to be challenged by brilliant UXers and work with finance experts. I learned a lot about stakeholder management and how to communicate design decisions. It helps that I received, and still do, positive feedback from both users and former coworkers.
