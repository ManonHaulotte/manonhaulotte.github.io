Corporate banking app: a redesign gone wrong.

Belfius is the leading Belgian bank, and their private banking app ranks high with customers, thanks to a great design team.

However, one department was having issues: the PCB team, who develops and maintains the digital solutions for corporate and public banking. They had been collaborating with an external agency to redesign their web app for the past 2 years. It should be noted that we're talking about a platform that people in various financial roles (accountants, CFO's, investments managers,â€¦) and in companies ranging from small local businesses to multimillion international corporations need to use to complete their daily tasks. An unclear navigation or poor user experience therefore means time lost during the work day, constant frustration for the employees and real financial consequences for our clients.

Unfortunately, the redesign had prioritized the look and feel, with little to no consideration for user needs and context. The big launch was not going as planned: users were confused by the new version of BelfiusWeb, the support team was receiving calls everyday and corporate clients weren't happy.

That's when I was brought on board. It was the first time a UX designer would work with this team and on this product.

For a month, I mostly observed. I worked on small design tasks, while getting to know the many stakeholders, asking many questions to the user researchers and the customers support team, learning from the domain experts and familiarizing myself with the complex and multilayered platform. It became obvious that the product reflected the team organization: different stakeholders with varying visions were working in silos, domain and user knowledge was scattered accross departments, and a proper design process needed to be formalized. First, I got the team together, with some key external stakeholders, so they could define five design principles for BelfiusWeb. Not only was this a fun workshop to facilitate, it created a shared design vision that everyone felt they could own, introduced a different way of working and proved tremendously helpful with decision making later on.
